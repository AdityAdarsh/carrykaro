Safety & Prohibited Items

Last updated: July 15, 2026

CarryKaro connects senders and travellers directly — we don't inspect, X-ray, or take custody of any package. That means the two of you are meeting as strangers, and the safety of that handoff is in your hands. Read this before you meet.

The three rules: Meet in a public place. Inspect the package openly before accepting it. Never accept a sealed or taped package you haven't seen inside.

1. Meet in Public

  • Choose a busy, well-lit public location for handoff — a mall, a metro/railway station concourse, a café. Avoid private homes or secluded spots for a first meeting.
  • If either party feels unsafe or pressured, you can walk away and decline the match at any time — you're not obligated to complete a handoff that feels wrong.
  • Tell a friend or family member where you're going and when, especially for first-time matches.

2. No Sealed Packages — Open-Package Policy

  • Travellers: do not accept a sealed, taped, or wrapped package. Ask the sender to open it in front of you so you can see exactly what you're carrying.
  • Senders: bring your item unsealed (or easily openable) to the handoff, and be ready to show its contents.
  • If a sender refuses to show what's inside, do not accept the delivery. Decline the match and report it to us at adityai81011@gmail.com.
  • This protects the traveller: under Indian law, a person carrying an unknown package can be held responsible for its contents. Seeing what you carry is your protection, not just a courtesy.

3. Prohibited Items

The following must never be sent or carried through CarryKaro:

  • Cash, currency, or negotiable financial instruments
  • Illegal drugs, narcotics, or controlled substances of any kind
  • Firearms, ammunition, weapons, or explosives
  • Alcohol or tobacco products (unless both parties independently hold all legally required licenses — CarryKaro does not verify this)
  • Stolen goods or counterfeit products
  • Live animals or perishable food requiring refrigeration
  • Hazardous materials — flammable, corrosive, radioactive, or toxic substances
  • Any item whose possession or transport is restricted or illegal under Indian law

Sending or carrying a prohibited item is a violation of our terms and may expose you to criminal liability under Indian law. CarryKaro is not a courier or logistics company, does not inspect packages, and bears no responsibility for the contents of any item exchanged between users.

4. If Something Goes Wrong

  • CarryKaro currently has no in-app payments, escrow, or insurance — do not send money to the other party outside of what you've agreed for the delivery itself.
  • If a match feels unsafe, a package doesn't match its description, or someone asks you to carry a prohibited item, stop the handoff and report it to adityai81011@gmail.com.
  • We can suspend accounts that violate these guidelines, but we cannot undo a completed handoff — the rules above exist so nothing goes wrong in the first place.

5. Your Responsibility

By using CarryKaro, you agree that you are solely responsible for ensuring any item you send or carry is legal, accurately described, and safe. CarryKaro facilitates introductions between senders and travellers; it is not a party to the delivery itself.
